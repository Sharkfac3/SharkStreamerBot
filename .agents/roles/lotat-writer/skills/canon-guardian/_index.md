# Canon Guardian — lotat-writer

## When to Activate This Sub-Skill

- Reviewing a new LotAT adventure for canon consistency before it is finalized
- Adding new world elements, locations, hazards, factions, or recurring story motifs to LotAT
- Writing story content that leans heavily on established characters, ship regions, or Starship Shamples lore
- Checking whether a proposed story expansion belongs in LotAT at all or should be escalated to broader canon review

## Purpose

This is the **LotAT-specific canon review path**. Use it to validate story content against the established Starship Shamples universe **as it appears in live adventure content**.

This sub-skill focuses on:
- story pacing and live-session fit
- correct use of the existing cast
- consistency with established world rules and Chaos Meter behavior
- avoiding accidental lore drift in reusable story content

For broader brand/cast/metaphor changes, escalate to `brand-steward` canon review.

## LotAT Canon Questions

Answer these before approving new story content:

**1. Does the story respect LotAT format rules?**
- Is the adventure paced for live stream play?
- Do stage beats stay readable at stream speed?
- Does the structure match the established LotAT story format and command constraints?

**2. Does it preserve world logic?**
- Do locations, hazards, and choices fit the existing Starship Shamples universe?
- Does the story introduce anything that behaves like permanent lore without explicit approval?
- If a new world element appears, is it framed as a local/adventure-specific element rather than franchise-wide canon unless explicitly intended?

**3. Do characters behave correctly?**
- Does each recurring character act like their Character Codex version?
- Is any commander moment rare, distinctive, and personality-accurate?
- Is the story accidentally giving a character a new permanent role, trait, or relationship?

**4. Does the Chaos Meter stay honest?**
- Does chaos escalate rather than reset mid-story?
- Do endings reflect the final chaos state?
- Are dice hooks and commander moments used sparingly rather than constantly?

**5. Does this create broader canon drift?**
- Would this story force updates to `Creative/Brand/CHARACTER-CODEX.md`?
- Would this imply a new permanent ship section, recurring faction, or named cast member?
- Does this change the metaphor layer rather than simply expressing it through story?

## Review Checklist

- [ ] Story structure matches LotAT pacing and schema expectations
- [ ] No unsupported commands or schema inventions are implied
- [ ] Existing characters remain in-character
- [ ] Chaos Meter progression remains consistent
- [ ] New lore is either temporary/local or explicitly approved for canon expansion
- [ ] Story tone stays absurd, chaotic, humorous, and stream-playable
- [ ] Anything with wider franchise impact is flagged for `brand-steward-canon-guardian`

## Escalate to brand-steward canon review when

- adding or materially changing a recurring character
- introducing a permanent ship section or franchise-level setting rule
- changing how a character maps to the neurodivergent metaphor
- creating lore that would require updates to canonical brand or character docs

Canonical escalation target: `.pi/skills/brand-steward-canon-guardian/SKILL.md`
