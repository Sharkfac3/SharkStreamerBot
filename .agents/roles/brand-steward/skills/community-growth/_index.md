# Community Growth — brand-steward

## What This Skill Covers

Strategy for growing and engaging the community across platforms (Discord, Twitch, social media). The community is not just the audience — it is the future customer base. Community growth serves the full business pipeline: entertainment → engagement → trust → products.

## Core Principles

1. **Community as crew, not audience** — growth strategy must maintain the participatory culture; scaling should not make the community feel like passive consumers
2. **Knowledge sharing drives authority** — sharing R&D knowledge freely is the primary growth lever; people come for free expertise and stay for the community
3. **Entertainment retains** — mini-games, commanders, and LotAT stories keep people watching through slow stretches of real work; retention feeds growth
4. **Short-form feeds discovery** — clip-worthy moments from stream become short-form content that reaches people who will never watch live; discovery feeds community
5. **Discord deepens engagement** — the Discord community is where casual viewers become invested members who eventually become customers

## Growth Pipeline

| Stage | Platform | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| Discovery | YouTube Shorts, TikTok, Instagram | Short-form content from stream highlights (chain to `content-repurposer`) |
| First visit | Twitch live stream | Entertainment layers hook first-time viewers; real work keeps them curious |
| Return visits | Twitch live stream | Commanders, mini-games, LotAT create appointment viewing and participation |
| Community join | Discord | Regulars join Discord for deeper engagement; knowledge sharing continues |
| Investment | Discord + Twitch | Members feel ownership through participation; they watched the products get built |
| Customer | Product launch | Community that is already invested and trusts the brand becomes the natural customer base |

## When Working in This Skill

- Always read `Creative/Brand/BRAND-IDENTITY.md` first — growth strategy must align with brand pillars
- Growth should never feel like marketing — it should feel like the community growing naturally because the content is genuinely worth sharing
- Avoid growth-hack language ("funnel," "convert," "monetize" in public-facing copy) — the community should never feel like they're being optimized
- Consider how growth initiatives create or support clip-worthy moments for the content pipeline
