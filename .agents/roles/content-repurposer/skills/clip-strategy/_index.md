# Clip Strategy — content-repurposer

## What This Skill Covers

How to identify, select, and prioritize clip-worthy moments from stream recordings. This is the critical first step in the content pipeline — if the wrong moments are selected, everything downstream is wasted effort.

## Clip Selection Process

1. **Watch for peaks** — moments of high chat activity, emotional spikes (laughter, surprise, frustration), or visual drama often signal clip-worthy moments
2. **Categorize** — is this technical, entertainment, or both? This determines platform strategy and audience
3. **Evaluate standalone value** — would someone who has never seen this stream understand why this moment is interesting? If not, does it need context framing?
4. **Check duration** — platform requirements vary, but most short-form content works best at 30-60 seconds; identify natural start/end points
5. **Prioritize** — not every good moment needs to be clipped; select the moments that best represent the brand and serve the content calendar

## Proactive Clip Strategy

Beyond reviewing recordings, this skill also covers proactive work: identifying opportunities for OTHER roles to create clip-worthy moments.

Examples:
- Suggest to `streamerbot-dev` that a new mini-game mechanic would create visually dramatic moments
- Suggest to `lotat-writer` that a story arc should build toward a specific type of climactic moment
- Note to `brand-steward` that certain community engagement patterns create organic entertaining moments
